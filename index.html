Brief History of Jean Childs Young

Jean Childs Young Middle School is named in honor of Jean Childs Young, a distinguished educator, civil rights activist, and advocate for children and families. Mrs. Young was widely recognized for her work in education and her dedication to improving opportunities for young people. She was also the wife of Andrew Young, former Mayor of Atlanta and United States Ambassador to the United Nations, whose legacy of public service continues to inspire the Atlanta community.

The school reflects her commitment to academic achievement, equity, and community involvement. Since its founding, Jean Childs Young Middle School has served as a cornerstone of education in Atlanta Public Schools, emphasizing both academic rigor and character development. Today, the school continues to uphold her legacy by empowering students to become lifelong learners and leaders in their communities.
